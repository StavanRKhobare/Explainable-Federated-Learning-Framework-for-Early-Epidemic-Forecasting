1. Introduction:
This project focuses on developing an Federated Learning Framework for
Early Epidemic Forecasting to predict dengue and malaria outbreaks in
advance using privacy-preserving artificial intelligence techniques.
Existing surveillance systems are largely reactive and often detect
outbreaks only after widespread transmission. Moreover, healthcare data
is distributed across multiple institutions and cannot be centrally shared
due to privacy constraints.
The proposed system integrates federated learning, temporal graph neural
networks, privacy-aware preprocessing, and explainable AI mechanisms
to collaboratively learn from distributed healthcare datasets without
exposing sensitive patient information. By combining epidemiological
data, environmental factors, and hospital network data, the framework
aims to generate early outbreak alerts and interpretable insights for public
health intervention.
2. Problem Definition:
2.1. Problem Statement:
Existing outbreak prediction systems for dengue and malaria are limited
by centralized learning approaches, lack of privacy preservation,
insufficient spatio-temporal modelling, and minimal explainability. These
limitations make it difficult to generate early, accurate, and interpretable
outbreak forecasts using distributed healthcare data from multiple
institutions.
This project aims to develop a privacy-preserving and explainable
framework capable of forecasting epidemic outbreaks at an early stage.
2.2. Background Information:
Vector-borne diseases such as dengue and malaria continue to pose
significant global and national public health challenges. Their spread is
influenced by multiple factors including weather conditions, population
density, regional spread patterns, and hospital case trends.
Recent advances in federated learning, graph neural networks, and
explainable AI make it possible to address privacy and interpretability
challenges in healthcare analytics. Integrating these technologies into a
unified forecasting framework can significantly improve proactive
outbreak detection and intervention.

3. Objectives:
3.1. Primary Objectives:
• To develop a privacy-preserving federated learning framework for
early epidemic forecasting.
• To design a spatio-temporal graph neural network model for
outbreak risk prediction.
• To incorporate explainability mechanisms for interpretable risk
insights.
• To integrate multi-source datasets including epidemiological,
weather, and hospital data.
• To provide dashboard-based outbreak alerts and hotspot
visualization.
3.2. Secondary Objectives:
• To improve proactive public health decision-making
• To support better healthcare resource allocation
• To reduce delayed outbreak response
• To create a scalable AI-driven disease surveillance framework
4. Methodology:
The project follows a layered AI-driven methodology combining
preprocessing, graph-based learning, federated aggregation, and
explainability.
